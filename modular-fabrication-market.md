<p><p>The modular fabrication market consists of two primary types: permanent and relocatable structures. Permanent modular buildings are designed for long-term use, integrating seamlessly into established environments, often seen in commercial, residential, and educational settings. Conversely, relocatable modular units are designed for short-term applications and can be easily moved or reconfigured, making them ideal for construction sites, temporary offices, or disaster relief housing. Both types offer flexibility, speed of construction, and sustainability in response to various market needs.</p></p>
