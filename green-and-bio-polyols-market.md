<p><p>The green and bio polyols market consists of polyether polyols and polyester polyols, both derived from renewable resources. Polyether polyols are primarily produced through the polymerization of epoxides, offering flexibility and durability in applications like flexible foams and coatings. In contrast, polyester polyols are synthesized from the reaction of diols and dicarboxylic acids, providing superior strength and thermal stability, making them suitable for rigid foams and elastomers. Both types support sustainable practices by reducing reliance on fossil fuels.</p></p>
