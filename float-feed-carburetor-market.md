In contrast, the Diaphragm Carburetor uses a flexible diaphragm to modulate fuel flow, providing improved responsiveness in varying operating conditions. Both types serve critical roles in engine performance, with float-feed carburetors often preferred for traditional applications and diaphragm carburetors favored for modern engines requiring precision fuel delivery.</p></p>
