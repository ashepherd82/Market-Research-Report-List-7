High-frequency X-ray generators operate at elevated frequencies, offering improved image quality, reduced exposure time, and enhanced patient comfort due to lower radiation doses. They are commonly used in various diagnostic imaging applications. In contrast, low-frequency X-ray generators utilize lower frequencies, resulting in longer exposure times and potentially higher radiation doses.
