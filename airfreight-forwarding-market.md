<p><ul><li>Full Charter</li><li>Partial Charter</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The airfreight forwarding market comprises two primary types: full charter and partial charter. In a full charter, an entire aircraft is dedicated to transporting goods for a single client, providing maximum capacity and direct service but typically at higher costs. Conversely, a partial charter involves sharing an aircraft with other shipments, allowing for lower rates and more flexible capacity utilization. Both options cater to diverse logistical needs, balancing cost efficiency with service requirements in the global supply chain.</p></p>
