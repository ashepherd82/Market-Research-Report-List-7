<p><ul><li>Electrical Double Layer Capacitors</li><li>Pseudocapacitor</li><li>Asymmetric Supercapacitor</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Graphene-based supercapacitors are categorized into three main types: Electrical Double Layer Capacitors (EDLCs), Pseudocapacitors, and Asymmetric Supercapacitors. EDLCs utilize the electrostatic charge accumulation at the electrode interface, offering high power density and long cycle life. Pseudocapacitors involve fast redox reactions, providing higher energy density than EDLCs. Asymmetric supercapacitors combine features of both, with one electrode functioning like an EDLC and the other like a pseudocapacitor, resulting in a balanced performance ideal for various energy storage applications.</p></p>
