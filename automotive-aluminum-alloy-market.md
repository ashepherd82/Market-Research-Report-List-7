<p><p>The automotive aluminum alloy market comprises three main types: cast, rolled, and extruded aluminum alloys. Cast aluminum alloys are produced through pouring molten metal into molds, ideal for complex shapes like engine blocks. Rolled aluminum alloys are created by deforming metal sheets through rolling processes, commonly used for panels and structural components. Extruded aluminum alloys are formed by forcing molten metal through a die, perfect for creating long, consistent profiles like frames and supports. Each type serves distinct applications in vehicle manufacturing.</p></p>
